BANANA BREAD PANCAKES

Fluffy, cozy, and full of banana bread flavor, these pancakes are the perfect weekend treat.
The edges get golden and buttery while the centers stay soft and full of melty chocolate chips.
Stack them high, drizzle with maple syrup, and watch them disappear in seconds.


Prep Time: 10 mins               Cook Time: 20 mins
Total Time: 30 mins               Servings: 4

Ingredients

  • 11/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 3 tablespoon brown sugar
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 large egg
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted (plus more for pan)
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cup cold buttermilk
  • 2 ripe banana, mashed
  • 1/2 cup walnuts
  • 1/4 cup chocolate chips
FOR SERVING
  • maple syrup

INSTRUCTIONS

  1. In a large bowl, mash the ripe bananas until mostly smooth with a few small chunks left.

  2. Whisk in the egg, melted butter, vanilla extract, and buttermilk until combined.

  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together the flour, brown sugar, baking powder, baking soda, cinnamon, and salt.

  4. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry and use a rubber spatula to gently fold until just combined.
    Don’t overmix — a few lumps in the batter are perfect.

  5. Heat a nonstick pan or griddle over medium heat and add about 1/2 tablespoon of butter to melt.

  6. Scoop about 1/2 cup of batter for each pancake, or more depending on how big or small you like them.
    Once the batter hits the pan, sprinkle a few walnuts and mini chocolate chips right on top before flipping.

  7. Cook until bubbles form on top and edges look set, then flip and cook the other side until golden brown.

  8. Transfer to a plate and keep warm, then repeat with the remaining batter, adding more butter to the pan as needed.

  9. Serve warm with butter, maple syrup, and an extra sprinkle of chocolate chips.
